## Classroom Technology Overview

SAS classrooms are equipped with state-of-the-art audio-visual technology to enhance teaching and learning. Each classroom includes Apple TVs, projectors, and an Optvio controller for managing all devices.

<Note>
  **Standard Classroom Setup**: Apple TV for wireless screen sharing, projector for display, and Optvio controller for managing all classroom AV devices.
</Note>

## Apple TV for Screen Sharing

### Sharing Your Screen

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="iPad">
    **Share iPad Screen to Apple TV:**

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Swipe Down Control Center">
        Swipe down from top-right corner (or swipe up on older iPads)
      </Step>

      <Step title="Tap Screen Mirroring">
        Tap the **Screen Mirroring** button
      </Step>

      <Step title="Select Classroom Apple TV">
        Choose your classroom's Apple TV from the list

        Example: "Room 205 Apple TV"
      </Step>

      <Step title="Enter Code (if prompted)">
        If an AirPlay code appears on screen, enter it on your iPad
      </Step>

      <Step title="Start Teaching">
        Your iPad screen now appears on the projector!
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    **Stop Sharing:**
    - Open Control Center → Screen Mirroring → Stop Mirroring
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="MacBook">
    **Share MacBook Screen to Apple TV:**

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Click AirPlay Icon">
        Click the **AirPlay** icon in menu bar (top-right)

        Look for: <Icon icon="tv" />
      </Step>

      <Step title="Select Apple TV">
        Choose your classroom Apple TV from the dropdown list
      </Step>

      <Step title="Choose Display Mode">
        **Mirror Display**: Shows same content on both screens

        **Extend Display**: Uses Apple TV as second monitor
      </Step>

      <Step title="Enter Code (if needed)">
        Enter AirPlay code if prompted on the projector
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    **Stop Sharing:**
    - Click AirPlay icon → Disconnect AirPlay Display

    <Tip>
      **Presenter Mode**: Use "Extend Display" to show presentation on projector while viewing notes on your MacBook!
    </Tip>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="iPhone (Staff)">
    **Share iPhone Screen:**

    1. Open **Control Center** (swipe down from top-right)
    2. Tap **Screen Mirroring**
    3. Select classroom Apple TV
    4. Enter code if prompted

    **Use Cases:**
    - Share photos or videos
    - Demonstrate mobile apps
    - Show websites on mobile view
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Apple TV Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can't see the Apple TV in list" icon="circle-exclamation">
    **Not finding your classroom Apple TV?**

    1. **Check WiFi connection:**
       - Ensure you're connected to SAS WiFi
       - Apple TV must be on same network
    2. **Check Apple TV is powered on:**
       - Use Optvio controller to power on
       - Look for Apple TV icon/home screen on projector
    3. **Restart your device:**
       - Turn WiFi off and on
       - Restart iPad or MacBook
    4. **Check AirPlay settings:**
       - Settings → AirPlay should be enabled
       - Apple TV may need restart

    **Still not working?** Contact [edtech@sas.edu.sg](mailto:edtech@sas.edu.sg)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Screen mirroring is laggy or slow" icon="gauge">
    **Performance issues:**

    1. **Move closer to WiFi access point** - Better signal = smoother mirroring
    2. **Close unnecessary apps** - Free up device resources
    3. **Check network congestion** - Many devices on WiFi can slow performance
    4. **Restart Apple TV** - Use Optvio to power cycle
    5. **Use wired connection** - Some classrooms have HDMI cables for direct connection

    **For presentations:** Consider using HDMI cable for lag-free display
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="AirPlay code keeps appearing" icon="key">
    **Authentication required every time:**

    This is a security feature, but can be adjusted:

    1. **Contact EdTech** to adjust Apple TV settings for your room
    2. **Verify correct Apple TV** - You might be connecting to wrong room
    3. **Network settings** - May require network profile update

    Email [edtech@sas.edu.sg](mailto:edtech@sas.edu.sg) for assistance
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Optvio AV Controller

### What is Optvio?

**Optvio** is the central control system in your classroom for managing all audio-visual equipment. It provides a simple touch interface to control projectors, Apple TVs, sound systems, and more.

<Info>
  **Learn More**: Visit [Optvio's website](https://optvio.com/product-category/controller/) for detailed product information.
</Info>

### Using the Optvio Controller

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Basic Controls">
    **Power & Source Management:**

    **Turn On System:**
    1. Tap **Power On** button on Optvio controller
    2. All connected devices power on automatically
    3. Default source (usually Apple TV) appears on screen

    **Switch Input Source:**
    1. Tap **Source** or **Input** button
    2. Select desired input:
       - Apple TV (wireless sharing)
       - HDMI (wired connection)
       - Document Camera (if available)
       - Computer (VGA/HDMI)

    **Adjust Volume:**
    - Use volume slider or +/- buttons
    - Mute button for quick silence

    **Power Off:**
    1. Tap **Power Off** or **System Off**
    2. All devices shut down gracefully
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Multiple Screen Control">
    **Share to Multiple Displays:**

    Some classrooms support sharing content to multiple screens:

    **Enable Multiple Screens:**
    1. Open Optvio controller interface
    2. Tap **Multi-Screen** or **Display Layout**
    3. Select which screens to activate:
       - Main projector
       - Secondary display
       - Student monitors (if available)

    **Display Modes:**
    - **Mirror**: Same content on all screens
    - **Extend**: Different content on each screen
    - **Split**: Divide content across screens

    **Use Cases:**
    - Show presentation on front, instructions on side screen
    - Display student work on multiple screens
    - Create interactive learning stations

    <Tip>
      **Collaboration**: Multiple students can share to different screens simultaneously for presentations!
    </Tip>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Advanced Features">
    **Additional Optvio Features:**

    **Presets:**
    - Save favorite AV configurations
    - One-tap to activate common setups
    - Example: "Presentation Mode", "Group Work Mode"

    **Device Control:**
    - Control individual devices separately
    - Adjust brightness, contrast, audio settings
    - Switch between different audio sources

    **Scheduling:**
    - Some systems support scheduled power on/off
    - Automatic source switching
    - Contact EdTech to configure

    **Troubleshooting:**
    - Built-in diagnostics
    - Device status indicators
    - Reset options for frozen devices
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Optvio Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Controller not responding" icon="tablet-screen-button">
    **Optvio controller frozen or unresponsive:**

    1. **Check power:**
       - Verify controller is powered on
       - Check if screen is lit
    2. **Restart controller:**
       - Press and hold power button (if available)
       - Or unplug power for 10 seconds
    3. **Check network connection:**
       - Controller needs network to communicate with devices
       - Verify Ethernet cable is connected
    4. **Manual device control:**
       - Use physical power buttons on projector/Apple TV temporarily

    **Controller won't restart?** Contact [edtech@sas.edu.sg](mailto:edtech@sas.edu.sg) or [ithelp@sas.edu.sg](mailto:ithelp@sas.edu.sg)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Projector won't turn on" icon="video">
    **Projector power issues:**

    1. **Try Optvio power cycle:**
       - Turn off all devices via Optvio
       - Wait 30 seconds
       - Turn back on
    2. **Check projector directly:**
       - Look for LED indicator light on projector
       - Green = Ready, Red = Standby, Orange = Warming up
    3. **Check cables:**
       - Verify all cables are securely connected
       - Check power cable to projector
    4. **Lamp/bulb status:**
       - Projector lamp may need replacement
       - Check for error messages on screen

    **Persistent issues?** Submit ticket to [ithelp@sas.edu.sg](mailto:ithelp@sas.edu.sg)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="No sound from speakers" icon="volume-xmark">
    **Audio troubleshooting:**

    1. **Check Optvio volume:**
       - Volume may be muted
       - Increase volume slider
    2. **Verify audio source:**
       - Ensure correct input selected
       - Apple TV vs HDMI may have different audio settings
    3. **Check device volume:**
       - iPad/MacBook volume should be at reasonable level
       - Unmute device if necessary
    4. **Audio output settings:**
       - MacBook: Check System Settings → Sound → Output
       - iPad: Check audio is routed to AirPlay
    5. **Speaker connection:**
       - Verify speakers are powered on
       - Check if speaker cables connected to controller

    **No audio?** Contact [edtech@sas.edu.sg](mailto:edtech@sas.edu.sg)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Wrong input source showing" icon="right-left">
    **Input/Source selection:**

    1. **Use Optvio to switch source:**
       - Tap **Source** or **Input** button
       - Select correct source (Apple TV, HDMI, etc.)
    2. **Cycle through inputs:**
       - Some controllers have "Input +" button
       - Press multiple times to cycle
    3. **Reset to default:**
       - Power off all via Optvio
       - Power back on (defaults to primary source)
    4. **Check device is on:**
       - Source device (Apple TV, computer) must be powered on

    **Can't switch sources?** EdTech can help: [edtech@sas.edu.sg](mailto:edtech@sas.edu.sg)
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Classroom Technology Best Practices

<Tip>
  **Using Classroom Technology Effectively:**

  ✅ **Test before class** - Arrive early to test all AV systems

  ✅ **Power off when done** - Use Optvio to shut down all devices at end of day

  ✅ **Report issues immediately** - Don't wait for problems to get worse

  ✅ **Keep controllers accessible** - Don't block or cover Optvio controller

  ✅ **Use wireless when possible** - AirPlay is easier than cables

  ✅ **Have backup plan** - Keep HDMI cable handy in case wireless fails

  ✅ **Manage WiFi load** - Disconnect when not actively sharing screen
</Tip>

### Teaching Tips

**Effective Use of Classroom Displays:**

1. **Pre-load content:**
   - Have presentations open before class
   - Pre-connect to Apple TV
   - Test videos/media in advance

2. **Student engagement:**
   - Allow students to share their work via AirPlay
   - Use multiple screens for group presentations
   - Display student work for class feedback

3. **Interactive lessons:**
   - Share iPad with interactive apps
   - Use Apple Pencil on iPad to annotate on projected screen
   - Collaborate in real-time with Google apps

4. **Accessibility:**
   - Increase font sizes for better visibility
   - Use high-contrast mode for students with visual needs
   - Position displays to avoid glare

## Wired Connections (HDMI)

### When to Use HDMI Cable

While wireless AirPlay is convenient, sometimes a wired connection is better:

**Use HDMI cable when:**
- ✅ Presenting video with audio (no lag)
- ✅ Wireless is slow or unstable
- ✅ Playing high-resolution media
- ✅ Demonstrating fast-paced content (gaming, animation)
- ✅ Privacy needed (wired can't be seen by others)

**Connecting HDMI:**

1. Locate HDMI cable in classroom (usually at teacher desk)
2. Connect to your device:
   - **MacBook**: May need USB-C to HDMI adapter
   - **iPad**: Need Lightning to HDMI adapter (check with EdTech)
   - **Windows**: Usually built-in HDMI port
3. Use Optvio controller to select **HDMI** input source
4. Adjust display settings on your device if needed
