## High School BYOD Program

High School students at SAS (Grades 9-12) are **required** to provide their own laptop that meets minimum specifications. This BYOD (Bring Your Own Device) program prepares students for college and career success.

<Warning>
  **Mandatory Program**: All students in Grades 9-12 must have a laptop that meets or exceeds SAS minimum specifications.
</Warning>

## Why BYOD?

The Bring Your Own Device program:

- **Prepares for college** - Students learn to manage their own technology
- **Personalizes learning** - Choose the device that fits your workflow
- **Builds responsibility** - Ownership develops accountability
- **Supports independence** - High school students ready for self-management
- **Enables creativity** - Freedom to customize and optimize for your needs

<Note>
  **Technology Philosophy**: "Technology is used to enhance learning and make it more personalized, collaborative, and relevant."
</Note>

### Recommended Specifications

For optimal performance, consider:

- **RAM:** 8GB or more
- **Storage:** 512GB SSD (faster than HDD)
- **Processor:** Intel i5/i7 or Apple M1/M2 or AMD Ryzen 5/7
- **Display:** 13-15 inch screen (portable yet usable)
- **Battery Life:** 8+ hours for full school day
- **Build Quality:** Durable for daily transport

<Tip>
  **Recommendation**: MacBook Air or MacBook Pro are popular choices and align with SAS Elementary/Middle School programs, but Windows laptops meeting specifications work equally well!
</Tip>

## Frequently Asked Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What if I can't afford a laptop?" icon="hand-holding-dollar">
    **Financial Assistance:**

    If purchasing a laptop creates financial hardship:

    1. Contact School Administration or Counseling Office
    2. Discuss financial assistance options
    3. Possible support or loaner programs may be available
    4. Confidential conversation

    SAS is committed to ensuring all students have access to necessary technology.

    <Note>
      Don't let financial concerns prevent you from asking for help - resources may be available.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I use a tablet instead of a laptop?" icon="tablet">
    **Tablet vs. Laptop:**

    **No - a tablet is not sufficient.**

    **Why laptops are required:**
    - Full keyboard for typing assignments
    - Powerful software for coursework (coding, design, etc.)
    - Multiple windows and multitasking
    - File management and organization
    - Standardized testing requirements
    - College/career preparation

    **iPad/Surface with keyboard?**
    - Generally not recommended
    - May struggle with specific software
    - Check with EdTech before purchasing
    - Traditional laptop preferred

    <Warning>
      Chromebooks may have limited compatibility with some coursework - verify with Tech Help Center before purchasing.
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What happens if my laptop breaks?" icon="laptop-medical">
    **Device Failure or Damage:**

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Get It Repaired">
        Take to manufacturer, retailer, or local repair shop for service
      </Step>

      <Step title="Request Loaner Chromebook">
        Visit Tech Help Center with proof of repair (receipt, email, etc.)
      </Step>

      <Step title="Use Loaner">
        Chromebook available for short-term use during repairs
      </Step>

      <Step title="Return Loaner">
        Return Chromebook when your laptop is repaired
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    **Timeline:**
    - Loaners available immediately
    - Can be kept during entire repair period
    - Must return when device is fixed or after 2 weeks

    **Your Responsibility:**
    - Arrange and pay for repairs
    - Backup data regularly (so you don't lose files)
    - Maintain device to prevent issues

    <Tip>
      AppleCare or extended warranties can save money on repairs - consider purchasing when buying your laptop!
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I upgrade my laptop mid-high school?" icon="arrow-up">
    **Upgrading Your Device:**

    **Yes, absolutely!**

    Students can upgrade to a new laptop at any time:

    **Reasons to Upgrade:**
    - Current laptop no longer meets needs
    - Newer technology available
    - Performance issues
    - Damaged beyond economical repair
    - Want better specs for advanced courses

    **Just ensure:**
    - New device meets minimum specifications
    - Backup all data before switching
    - Transfer necessary files
    - Set up accounts and apps on new device

    <Note>
      Many students upgrade after 10th or 11th grade, or when starting college-level courses.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Do I keep my laptop after graduation?" icon="graduation-cap">
    **After Graduation:**

    **Yes - it's yours to keep!**

    Since you purchased the laptop, it belongs to you:

    - Take it to college
    - Use for continued education
    - Keep for personal use
    - Sell or give to family member

    **Before You Leave:**
    - Backup all schoolwork to personal drive
    - Remove any school-managed software if desired
    - Keep for college - most universities require laptops
    - Update OS and software for continued use

    <Tip>
      The laptop you buy for 9th grade should last through your first year or two of college if you choose a good model!
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
